The Basics
6 questions
What is Skedee?
Most Asked

Skedee is Nigeria's number one prediction market. A platform where you can trade on the outcomes of real-world events using Naira.

Think of it like the stock market, but instead of trading shares in companies, you're trading on questions like "Will Man City win?" or "Will Burna Boy win a Grammy?" or "Will Peter Obi run in 2027?"

Every market is a YES/NO question. You buy YES if you think it will happen. You buy NO if you think it won't. Prices reflect what the crowd believes the probability is — and you can buy, sell, or exit your position at any time before the event ends.

How is this different from sports betting?

Regular betting apps set fixed odds and you bet against the house

On Skedee, you trade against other users Prices are set by market demand. If lots of people think Man City will win, the YES price rises. If doubt creeps in, it falls. Just like a stock price.

Key differences:

  • You can sell your position early and lock in profit before the match ends
  • Prices change in real time — you can react to live events
  • No bookmaker margin baked in — prices are fairer
  • You can go both ways — you can buy YES on one outcome and YES on another!
How do prices work? What does ₦74 mean?
Popular

Every market on Skedee is priced between ₦1 and ₦99. The price directly represents the market's current probability estimate.

So if a YES share costs ₦74, the market believes there's roughly a 74% chance that event happens. If it costs ₦22, the market thinks there's only a 22% chance.

If you buy YES at ₦74 and the event happens, each share pays out ₦100 — giving you a profit of ₦26 per share. If it doesn't happen, your shares expire worthless. The YES price and the NO price always add up to ₦100 (e.g. ₦74 YES + ₦26 NO = ₦100).

What can I trade on?

If it has a clear, verifiable outcome — there's likely a market for it on Skedee. Current categories include:

  • ⚽ Football — Premier League, AFCON, Champions League, NPFL
  • 🗳️ Nigerian Politics — Elections, governorship races, policy outcomes
  • 🎵 Music & Entertainment — Grammy nominations, album releases, chart performance
  • 🏀 Other Sports — NBA, tennis, F1, cricket
  • 📱 Tech & Business — Product launches, earnings, leadership changes

New markets are added every week. If you have an idea for a market, you can suggest it from the app.

Is Skedee legal in Nigeria?

Yes. Skedee operates as a regulated prediction market and holds the appropriate licenses to operate in Nigeria. We work closely with relevant regulatory bodies to ensure full compliance.

Prediction markets have a long global history as legitimate financial instruments. Unlike unregulated offshore betting sites, Skedee is a Nigerian company with Nigerian licensing, meaning your funds are protected under Nigerian law.

Skedee is only available to users aged 18 and over. We take responsible trading seriously — if you feel your activity is becoming a problem, please contact us and we will help.

Who can use Skedee?

Skedee is open to Nigerian residents aged 18 and over. You'll need a valid Nigerian bank account and phone number to sign up. We verify your identity as part of onboarding to keep the platform safe and compliant.

Users outside Nigeria can sign up but will need to complete additional verification. International deposits and withdrawals may have different processing times.

Account
4 questions
How do I create an account?
Quick

Creating an account takes under 2 minutes:

  • Tap Sign Up from any Skedee page
  • Enter your phone number and email
  • Verify your phone with a 6-digit OTP
  • Complete a quick identity check (BVN or NIN)
  • Fund your wallet and start trading

You'll be trading within minutes of signing up.

I forgot my password. How do I reset it?

On the login screen, tap "Forgot Password" and enter your registered email or phone number. You'll receive an OTP to reset your password within 60 seconds.

If you're not receiving the OTP, check your spam folder or contact support@skedee.ng.

Why do you need my BVN or NIN?

Nigerian financial regulations (CBN guidelines) require us to verify the identity of all users before they can make deposits or withdrawals. This is standard KYC (Know Your Customer) practice across all regulated Nigerian fintech platforms.

Your BVN or NIN is used only for identity verification — we do not have access to your bank account or any financial transactions through it. Your data is encrypted and never shared with third parties.

How do I close my account?

You can request account closure from Settings → Account → Close Account. Before closing, you'll need to withdraw any remaining balance and close all open positions.

Account closures are processed within 3 business days. If you change your mind, email support@skedee.ng within 24 hours of requesting closure.

Deposits & Withdrawals
5 questions
How do I deposit money into my wallet?
Easy

Deposits are processed instantly through Paystack, Nigeria's most trusted payment infrastructure. You can fund your wallet via:

  • Direct bank transfer (all Nigerian banks supported)
  • Debit card (Mastercard, Visa)
  • USSD (*737#, *822#, *966# etc)

The minimum deposit is ₦500. There are no fees on deposits — what you send is what lands in your wallet.

How do I withdraw my winnings? How long does it take?
Popular

Go to Wallet → Withdraw, enter the amount, and confirm with your transaction PIN. Withdrawals go directly to your linked Nigerian bank account.

Processing times:

  • Under ₦500,000 — usually instant, max 30 minutes
  • ₦500,000–₦5M — up to 2 hours
  • Above ₦5M — 1 business day, may require additional verification

The minimum withdrawal is ₦1,000. Withdrawals are free.

Is there a deposit or wallet limit?

Unverified accounts have a wallet limit of ₦50,000. Once you complete full KYC verification (BVN/NIN + selfie), your limit increases to ₦5,000,000.

High-volume traders can apply for a Pro account with no wallet cap. Contact support@skedee.ng to apply.

Do I need to pay tax on my winnings?

Skedee does not deduct tax at source. Whether your winnings are taxable under Nigerian law depends on your personal circumstances.

We recommend consulting a financial advisor for guidance specific to your situation. Skedee can provide a full transaction history for tax reporting purposes on request.

Trading
6 questions
How do I place a trade?

Find a market you want to trade. Tap BUY YES if you think the event will happen, or BUY NO if you think it won't.

Enter the amount in Naira you want to spend. The platform will show you exactly how many shares you'll receive and your estimated payout if you're right. Tap Place Order to confirm.

Your position will appear in your portfolio immediately. You can exit at any time before resolution by selling your shares.

Can I sell my position before the event ends?
Popular

Yes, always. This is one of the biggest differences between Skedee and traditional betting. You can exit any position at any time while the market is open.

If the price has moved in your favour since you bought, you can sell and get your profit early — even before the final whistle. If a market is moving against you, you can cut your losses before the event resolves.

Example: You buy Arsenal WIN YES at ₦55. Arsenal scores two quick goals and the market moves to ₦78. You can sell at ₦78 and take your ₦23-per-share profit immediately — without waiting for the game to end.

What happens if my trade is wrong?

If the event resolves against your position, your shares expire at ₦0 and you lose the amount you put in. You can never lose more than your initial stake — there is no margin or leverage on Skedee.

To minimise losses, you can always sell your position early if things aren't going your way. A partial recovery is better than a full loss.

Is there a maximum amount I can trade?

Position limits vary by market and are displayed on each market page. Most sports markets have a per-position limit of ₦500,000 for standard accounts. Political and long-term markets may have different limits.

Limits exist to ensure fair, liquid markets and to protect users from overexposure on a single event. Apply for a Pro account if you need higher limits.

What is the order book and spread?

The order book shows all open buy and sell orders in a market — who wants to buy YES at what price, and who wants to sell. It gives you a picture of market depth and liquidity.

The spread is the gap between the best current buy price and the best sell price. A ₦2 spread means the best YES offer is ₦2 above the best YES bid. Tighter spreads mean more liquid, more efficient markets. Skedee's most active markets typically have spreads of ₦1–₦3.

What is the Skedee Analyst in the Comments tab?
New Feature

The Skedee Analyst is an AI-powered market analyst built into every live match page. It monitors all market prices in real time and posts instant analysis when it detects significant price moves — like a sharp spike in the Penalty Awarded market or a sudden drop in a team's win probability.

You can also ask it questions directly by typing in the chat input. It knows the current score, all market prices, the match clock, and your open positions — so you can ask things like "Should I sell my City WIN position now?" or "What's driving the Red Card market spike?"

It's powered by Claude (Anthropic) and is for informational purposes only. It does not constitute financial advice.

Markets & Resolution
5 questions
How are markets resolved?
Popular

Every market has a Resolution Source listed on its page — this is the official data source we use to determine the outcome. For football markets, this is the official Premier League / competition data feed. For political markets, it's the official electoral commission announcement.

Markets resolve within 30 minutes of the final result being confirmed by the official source. Winnings are credited to your wallet instantly on resolution.

Resolution criteria are set before the market opens and cannot be changed once trading begins. You can always read the full resolution rules on each market's detail page.

What if the event is cancelled or postponed?

If an event is cancelled or postponed before it starts, all open positions are voided and your full stake is returned to your wallet — no fees charged.

If a match is abandoned mid-game, we follow the official competition rules. If the competition declares a void, the market voids. If the result stands (e.g. enough minutes played), the market resolves on that result.

Edge cases are always spelled out in each market's resolution rules. When in doubt, check before you trade.

What if I think a market was resolved incorrectly?

Contact support@skedee.ng within 24 hours of resolution with your dispute. Include the market name, the outcome you believe is correct, and any supporting evidence (e.g. official source link).

Our resolution team will review within 6 hours. If an error occurred, the market will be corrected and affected payouts recalculated. Skedee's final decision is binding.

Can I suggest a new market?

Yes! We love suggestions. Tap Suggest a Market from the sidebar in the app, or email your idea to markets@skedee.ng.

Good market suggestions have a clear YES/NO question, a verifiable data source for resolution, and a specific end date. If we launch your suggested market, you'll get a ₦500 wallet credit.

How long are markets open for trading?

Markets open as soon as they're listed — sometimes days or weeks before the event. Trading stays open right up until the moment the event begins (for pre-match markets) or throughout the event (for live in-play markets).

Live markets close automatically at full-time or when the event concludes. A market's open/close status is always shown on its page.

Safety & Security
2 questions
Is my money safe on Skedee?

Yes. All user funds are held in segregated escrow accounts with a CBN-licensed institution, completely separate from Skedee's operating funds. This means your money is protected even in the unlikely event that Skedee faces financial difficulties.

We use bank-grade 256-bit encryption for all data. Two-factor authentication (2FA) is available and strongly recommended for all accounts.

What if trading is becoming a problem for me?

We take responsible trading seriously. If you feel your trading activity is affecting your wellbeing or finances, please reach out — we will not judge you and we will help.

You can self-exclude from the platform at any time from Settings → Responsible Trading → Self-Exclusion. You can also set daily, weekly, or monthly deposit limits from the same menu.

For external support, you can contact the Lagos State Health Service Commission or the National Problem Gambling Helpline. Your health is more important than any trade.
