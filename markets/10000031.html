🇳🇬 Lagos Rain — May/June 2026
Lagos sits on the Atlantic coast and is in its peak rainy season right now. May averages 245mm across ~16 rain days — any single day has a better-than-coinflip chance of rain. The Saturday and Sunday single-day markets reflect this with 68% and 65% respectively.

The Both Days market is cheaper at 53% because it requires back-to-back rainfall. However, Lagos rain days are positively correlated — a wet Saturday significantly raises Sunday's probability, which is why the combined market trades higher than a naive P(Sat) × P(Sun) calculation would suggest.
📊 How the Three Markets Relate
Saturday (68%) and Sunday (65%) are independent single-day bets — whichever day you think is more likely to rain, you can target it directly. Both Days (53%) pays out only if you get rain on both, but the ₦53 entry price means you collect ₦100 for a ₦47 profit — better odds than the single-day markets relative to the extra risk.
Market Rules & Resolution
What counts as rain?
Measurable rainfall ≥ 1mm at the official NIMET Lagos station. Drizzle below 1mm does not count.
Resolution — Both Days
YES only if NIMET records ≥ 1mm in Lagos on both Sat May 30 AND Sun May 31.
Resolution — Saturday Only
YES if NIMET records ≥ 1mm in Lagos on Sat May 30 regardless of Sunday.
Resolution — Sunday Only
YES if NIMET records ≥ 1mm in Lagos on Sun May 31 regardless of Saturday.
Data source
NIMET Lagos official data, corroborated by Weather Underground or timeanddate.com Lagos.
