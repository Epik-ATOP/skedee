Where are prices now?
NNPCL's official pump price stands at ₦1,130/litre in Lagos as of May 16, 2026. Abuja and Port Harcourt are seeing ₦1,100-₦1,145 depending on station. The gap to ₦1,300 is just ₦170 — about two average monthly hikes at the current trajectory.
🛢️
What's driving prices up?
Brent crude is trading above $92/barrel, its highest in six months. NNPCL imports roughly 40% of refined petrol at market rates. With the naira at ₦1,720/$, the cost-reflective price formula keeps pushing the ceiling higher each review cycle.
🛑
What could keep it below?
NNPCL has historically absorbed losses in the weeks before politically sensitive dates. The administration may freeze the price adjustment until after the June 12 Democracy Day celebrations. A crude correction or naira appreciation could also widen the gap.

President Tinubu declared "subsidy is gone" on inauguration day. Petrol price jumped from ₦195/litre to ₦537 overnight — the single largest one-day increase in Nigerian history.
✅

Q3 2023
NNPCL restructured as a full commercial entity, empowered to adjust pump prices monthly based on crude cost, FX, and margin requirements. The era of fixed government pricing formally ended.

January 2026
After a series of quarterly hikes, Nigeria's petrol price crossed the symbolic ₦1,000 mark — triggering widespread protests and a brief NLC warning. Price settled at ₦1,050 after NNPCL absorbed some pressure.

NLC strike threat is credible — The Nigeria Labour Congress has already issued a 14-day ultimatum warning of a general strike if petrol crosses ₦1,300. This is a harder constraint than in previous cycles.
Crude could pull back — Brent's move above $92 was partly driven by short-term geopolitical noise. A resolution or demand-side softness could bring it back under $88, removing NNPCL's justification for a hike.

Market Rules & Resolution
Resolution Criteria
Resolves YES if the official NNPCL retail pump price at any NNPCL station in Nigeria reaches or exceeds ₦1,300 per litre before midnight on May 31, 2026. Resolves NO if the pump price remains strictly below ₦1,300 at close of market.
Resolution Source
NNPCL's official website (nnpcgroup.com) and verified pump price reports from the PPPRA. Nigerian downstream publications (Nairametrics, BusinessDay) are used as secondary verification.
What counts as "exceeds"?
An officially published NNPCL pump price of ₦1,300 or above at any retail outlet. Black-market or reseller prices do not count. The price must be an official NNPCL announcement.
What if prices vary by state?
If NNPCL officially sets different prices by region and any region hits ₦1,300 or above, the market resolves YES. A uniform national price below ₦1,300 resolves NO.
What if NNPCL announces after May 31?
Only price changes that take effect on or before May 31 count. An announcement on June 1 — even if backdated — does not trigger YES resolution.
