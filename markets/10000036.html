Market Rules
Resolution Condition
This market resolves YES if the Worldometer live population counter for Nigeria displays a figure of 250,000,000 or higher at any point before 23:59 WAT on December 31, 2026. Resolution is based on Worldometer's real-time estimate, which uses UN population projections with real-time birth/death rate extrapolation.

Data Source
Worldometer (worldometers.info/world-population/nigeria-population) — the same source used to establish the current baseline of ~242M. A screenshot with timestamp will be published at resolution. The UN World Population Prospects data may also be used as a secondary confirmation source.

Why It Probably Won't Happen
Nigeria's current net growth is approximately 16,600 people per day. To add 8 million people in the remaining ~209 days of 2026 would require an average of ~38,300 net new people per day — more than double the current rate. No credible demographic scenario supports this.

Why It Might (Long Shot)
Population estimates carry uncertainty. A revision to Nigeria's baseline figures — as has happened before with census data releases — could theoretically push the Worldometer estimate over 250M. The last Nigerian census was in 2006; any new official count could surprise.
