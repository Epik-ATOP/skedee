Where is the rainy season now?
Lagos is deep into the first rainy season (April–July). Ikeja has recorded 112mm so far in May — already 65% of the monthly average. June is historically Lagos's wettest month, averaging 330mm. The gap to the flood threshold is closing fast.
🏘️
Why does Lagos flood so badly?
Lagos is built on a network of lagoons and low-lying coastal land. Decades of blocked drainage channels, illegal structures on floodplains, and rapid population growth have made the city highly vulnerable. Key hotspots include Mile 12, Surulere, Bariga, Ikorodu, and Ojo LGAs — all repeatedly affected.
🛠️
What's the government doing?
Governor Sanwo-Olu's administration spent ₦12bn on flood control in 2025, including canal dredging and drainage clearance. LASEMA is on pre-emergency alert. However, inspections in April found 40% of dredged channels already re-blocked by refuse and illegal structures.

Lagos Flooding — Key Events
✅
2012 — The Great Lagos Flood HISTORIC
July 2012
The worst flooding in over 40 years. More than 365,000 people were displaced across 14 LGAs after record rainfall exceeded 380mm in a single month. Entire communities in Surulere, Bariga, and Ikorodu were submerged for days. It remains the benchmark for flood severity.
✅
2022 — Nationwide Flood Crisis DONE
September–October 2022
A catastrophic flood year driven by heavy rainfall and the release of water from the Lagdo Dam in Cameroon. Over 600 Nigerians were killed nationally. Lagos recorded 355mm in its peak month — 9 LGAs declared emergencies, and 120,000 residents were displaced in the state.
✅
2024 — NEMA Emergency Declared DONE
June–July 2024
NEMA declared an official flood emergency in Lagos after 8 LGAs were severely affected. 80,000+ residents were displaced. Governor Sanwo-Olu announced a ₦12bn emergency flood-control budget to be spent before the 2025 and 2026 rainy seasons.

Resolution Criteria
Resolves YES if NEMA (National Emergency Management Agency) or LASEMA (Lagos State Emergency Management Agency) officially declares a flood emergency affecting 3 or more Local Government Areas in Lagos State at any point before midnight on June 30, 2026. Resolves NO if no such declaration is made by that date.
Resolution Source
Official press releases from NEMA (nema.gov.ng) and LASEMA (lasema.gov.ng). Secondary verification via NTA, Channels TV, and major Nigerian newspapers (Punch, Guardian, Vanguard) where official government statements are reproduced verbatim.
What counts as a "major flood emergency"?
A formal written declaration by NEMA or LASEMA — not a press conference statement or social media post — that names 3 or more specific Lagos LGAs as flood emergency zones. The declaration must explicitly use emergency or disaster language and be verifiable from official government channels.
Does localised flooding in 1–2 LGAs count?
No. Flooding confined to 1 or 2 LGAs, however severe for residents in those areas, does not satisfy the resolution criteria. The 3-LGA threshold is intentional — it captures city-scale emergencies, not routine seasonal flooding that occurs almost every year.
What if LASEMA responds without declaring an emergency?
Operational flood response (deploying boats, evacuating residents, opening relief centres) without a formal written emergency declaration does not resolve YES. The formal declaration is the trigger.
