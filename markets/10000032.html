🇳🇬 Abuja Rain — June 2026
Abuja follows a tropical savanna climate — single rainy season from April to October, peaking in July/August. In early June the rains are building and becoming more reliable. The city averages ~128mm across ~13 rain days in May. Unlike Lagos's persistent Atlantic-driven moisture, Abuja rain comes from sporadic afternoon convective cells — local thunderstorms that form as solar heating peaks.

This makes Abuja rain harder to predict. A storm can dump 40mm in an hour, then nothing for two days. The Saturday and Sunday markets sit near 50/50, and the Both Days market at 32% reflects the low correlation between Abuja rain days compared to coastal Lagos.
📊 Abuja vs Lagos — Why Such Different Odds?
Lagos has 68%/65%/53% (Sat/Sun/Both). Abuja has 52%/49%/32%. The gap reflects the fundamental difference: Lagos has persistent onshore moisture making rain almost mechanical in peak season, while Abuja's inland position means each storm is an independent event. The Both Days gap (53% vs 32%) is even wider because Abuja rain days don't correlate like Lagos's do.
Market Rules & Resolution
What counts as rain?
Measurable rainfall ≥ 1mm at NIMET Abuja Airport station. Drizzle below 1mm does not count.
Resolution — Both Days
YES only if NIMET records ≥ 1mm in Abuja on both Sat Jun 6 AND Sun Jun 7.
Resolution — Saturday Only
YES if NIMET records ≥ 1mm in Abuja on Sat Jun 6 regardless of Sunday.
Resolution — Sunday Only
YES if NIMET records ≥ 1mm in Abuja on Sun Jun 7 regardless of Saturday.
