How it works
A daily yes/no market across 5 Lagos areas. pick on whether NEPA will bring light to each area by 10:00 PM. Each area is independent — win or lose per area based on the outcome.
📊
Manual odds
Odds are set daily based on each area's supply history. Reliable areas like Lekki have lower YES payouts. Notorious spots like Ikorodu offer higher multipliers if you're right.
🌙
Midnight resolution
At 10:00 PM WAT each market resolves. Was there light in that area? YES pays ₦100 per share. NO pays ₦100 per share. Only one side wins per area.
Lagos Electricity — Area Reputations
Lekki / VI — Generally the most consistent supply in Lagos. Proximity to independent power producers and preferential treatment means light is more likely than not on any given night.
Ikeja — The commercial capital of Lagos. Supply is moderate to good, though load shedding is common. Ikeja GRA and industrial estates often get priority.
Surulere — Classic middle-Lagos residential. Supply is roughly coin-flip — some days good, some days nothing for 18 hours. One of the most contested picking areas.
Yaba — Lagos's tech and university hub. Supply is moderate and fairly consistent, though student-heavy density means demand spikes. Generally a safe YES pick.
Apapa — The port district. Industrial demand competes with residential supply, making it unpredictable. DisCo priority can swing either way depending on port operations.
Lagos Island — The CBD. Banks, government offices, and commercial estates often get priority treatment. Supply is reasonably reliable, though the old grid infrastructure causes occasional failures.
VI (Victoria Island) — Lagos's financial and diplomatic hub. Supply here rivals Lekki — embassies, banks, and upscale estates mean it's often a priority circuit. Strong YES territory.
Agege — One of the most densely populated areas in Lagos. High demand, old transformers, and low DisCo investment make outages the norm. Strong NO territory.
Ikorodu — Notorious for extended outages. Supply here is genuinely unpredictable but tends to be poor. High-risk, high-reward territory for YES.
Badagry — The furthest from the grid's core. Badagry consistently reports some of the worst electricity access in the state. Brave pick if you go YES here.
Market Rules & Resolution
Resolution Criteria
Each area resolves YES if electricity is confirmed on at 11:59 PM WAT in that area — meaning lights are active in at least one household in the area at the close of market. Resolves NO if there is a confirmed blackout at the time of resolution, or if no reporter confirms power.
Resolution Source
Resolution is based on user reports submitted between 9:30 PM and 10:00 PM WAT. Reports require a photo or video of the reporter's electricity situation. Majority-vote determines the outcome. In the event of a tie, Skedee's editorial team makes the final call.
Odds & Payouts
Odds are manually set each morning by Skedee based on prior supply patterns and DisCo schedules. YES price × shares = stake. If correct, each share pays ₦100. Effective multiplier = ₦100 ÷ YES price (or NO price). Odds do not update intraday.
Daily Reset
A new market opens at 6:00 AM WAT each day. Each area's odds are freshly set each morning. Previous day results do not affect today's odds.
Disputes
Contact support@skedee.ng within 12 hours of resolution with photo/video evidence. Skedee's decision is final.
